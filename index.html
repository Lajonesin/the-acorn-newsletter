Water Conservation Program

Our Drips & Leaks Program runs March 10 – April 10, 2026. Water and sewer costs exceed $920,000 annually — our single largest utility expense. Sign up today — spots are first come, first served.

As part of this program, The Oaks will cover the cost of small plumbing repairs discovered during inspection — including running toilets, dripping faucets, and minor valve repairs. These repairs are provided at no charge to the resident.

Our team will contact you one day in advance to confirm a convenient time for your inspection and/or repair visit. Management reserves the right to determine if a repair is too extensive and does not guarantee any fix beyond what is promised. The Oaks is not responsible for any subsequent plumbing issues that are unrelated to the repair performed under this program.

📋 How to Sign Up

Spots are first come, first served. Inspections are available even if you don't think you have a leak — we find hidden leaks across all 556 units.

Community Rules & Violation Reminders
Top Enforcement Targets
  • Pets: Must be on a leash in common areas, cleaned up after immediately, and properly registered with the office.
  • Stairwells & stairs: Do not store personal belongings, plants, furniture, boxes, or bicycles under stairwells, on stairs, or on landings.
  • Noise & nuisance odors: Excessive noise and nuisance smells that interfere with neighbors may be treated as deed restriction violations.
  • Parking: Follow all parking and towing rules.
  • Vehicle stickers: Vehicles must display the proper Oaks sticker and current registration information as required.

Excessive noise and storage are specifically listed in the deed restriction violation policy.

Trash, Dumping & Common Areas
  • Do not leave trash bags, loose trash, pet waste, or household items outside where they attract rodents or other animals.
  • Trash left outside can create bad odors, look unsightly, and affect the whole community.
  • Do not dump furniture, mattresses, appliances, or bulky items in or around the regular dumpsters.
  • Large-item disposal is not free. Owners must contact the office, pay the required fee, and use the designated large trash dumpster area.
  • Improper dumping causes extra charges from the trash company that the community ultimately has to absorb.

Please help keep breezeways, stairs, and common areas cleaner, safer, and more attractive for everyone.

Important reminder: The Board has authority to adopt and enforce rules governing the administration, management, operation, and use of the property and common elements. The Association's deed restriction violation policy provides for an initial warning letter, followed by a $50 fine, with fines increasing by an additional $50 each month the infraction continues.

You Asked — Here's What We Heard

Questions raised by residents at the March 16, 2026 General Owner Meeting.

🚗 Parking & Towing Enforcement
If the towing company is not enforcing rules, will a new company be considered?

The Board is reviewing expectations with the current towing vendor. If compliance cannot be achieved, alternative vendors may be evaluated.

🔧 Unauthorized Modifications
What happens when a unit makes exterior or landscaping modifications without approval?

All exterior modifications require prior approval through the Architectural Review process. Regarding Unit 513 — the request was approved months prior and installation was delayed due to backorders. No unauthorized modification was identified.

🔥 Fire Hazards & Items Under Stairs
When will stored items (including pots on stairs) be removed?

This is a safety priority. The Board is coordinating removal timelines that comply with notice requirements. Residents will receive communication before enforcement begins.

📢 Communication Format
Can we return to clearer, more consistent announcement formatting?

The Board has received this feedback. Improvements to formatting and readability are being considered.

🐜 Sidewalk Ant Beds
What is being done about ant beds coming through sidewalk cracks?

This will be addressed as part of routine grounds maintenance. Please report specific locations through the resident portal.

💧 Water Conservation Program
Will you check units even if we don't think we have leaks?

Yes. Inspections are available even if no issue is visible. The goal is to find hidden leaks and reduce water waste.

How do we sign up?

Submit a request through the resident portal, call 713-972-1510, or email manager@theoaksofwoodlake.com. Spots are first come, first served.

🌿 Landscaping & Grounds
When will leaves be removed and will landscaping be more consistent?

Landscaping is being completed in rotating sections. Management has instructed the vendor to remove debris, not just redistribute it. The Board President will speak directly with the vendor about ongoing concerns.

Will overgrown shrubs and trees be trimmed?

Yes. Trimming is planned and ongoing.

🚿 Irrigation System
What is the status of the irrigation system?

A full inspection has been approved. The system is outdated and may reset improperly after power interruptions. The inspection will determine needed repairs and potential upgrades.

🔒 Security & Patrol
Will security enforcement be stricter?

Yes. Guards will check IDs, verify visitors, and increase visibility. After-hours access will include controlled entry and required verification stops.

🚨 Safety Concerns
Where did the gunshots come from?

Confirmed to be from a nearby park, not within the community.

What about the leaking transformer and armadillo holes?

The transformer has been reported to the city. For armadillo holes, please submit a work order through the resident portal.

🔨 Maintenance
What about dryer vent problems — will the HOA fix them?

Units will be inspected where accessible. Repairs are the responsibility of the individual unit owner, not the HOA.

🏡 Community Requests
Will the removed bench be replaced?

This item was not approved. It will be brought to a future Executive Session for discussion.
