Community Utility Awareness

Utilities are the largest expense for our community and are funded through monthly HOA dues. Because these costs are shared, overall usage directly impacts the Association's budget and future dues levels.

The Board evaluates these trends each year when preparing the budget. If overall utility usage continues to rise, those costs will need to be reflected in future HOA dues.

Residents are encouraged to take simple steps to reduce unnecessary usage and help keep expenses as controlled as possible.

Grilling Season Reminder

A friendly note as warm weather arrives

We love that residents enjoy grilling — just a few important reminders to keep our community safe and our grounds beautiful:

City of Houston Fire Code requires charcoal and gas grills to be kept a minimum of 10 feet from any structure. Grilling on balconies or patios attached to the building is not permitted.

Ash disposal: Allow ashes to cool fully (at least 48 hours), place in a metal container with a lid, and dispose of in the trash. Dumping ashes in flower beds, landscaping, or common areas is not appropriate and will result in fines.

Thank you for helping keep our community safe and looking its best!

A Note About Landscaping

Let's keep our grounds beautiful — together

We love seeing residents take pride in their surroundings — it truly shows how much our community means to you! That said, we want to make sure everyone is on the same page when it comes to our shared outdoor spaces.

Before planting flowers, adding potted plants, installing edging or fencing, or placing decorative items like statues, bird baths, or garden ornaments outside your unit, please check with the office first. We currently have an "Ask Before You Alter" form available, and a more comprehensive landscaping policy is on its way soon.

Tree and shrub trimming or removal is not permitted by residents. All tree and shrub work — including trimming, pruning, and removal — must be submitted as a request through the office and is handled exclusively by our licensed landscapers.

Our landscaping team works hard to keep the grounds looking their best, and when items are added or plants are altered without coordination, it can make their job more difficult and lead to areas that are tough to maintain over time.

We want to say yes as often as we can — we just need to know what's being planned! A quick call or email to the office is all it takes.

Starting soon, unapproved modifications to common or exterior areas may result in fines. 😊
